    "Do you want to work for a health insurer with below par standards and benefits for the non-management population?"

    StarStarStarStarStar
    • Work/Life Balance
    • Career Opportunities
    • Comp & Benefits
    • Senior Management
    Former Employee - Senior Financial Analyst in White Plains, NY (US)
    Former Employee - Senior Financial Analyst in White Plains, NY (US)
    Doesn't Recommend
    Disapproves of CEO

    Pros

    None, I would not recommend Aetna as a employer of choice.

    Cons

    In the five years in this position the highest merit increase received has been 1% with a overall scorecard rating of 4 out of 5. The gap between management and the non-management population in salary continues to grow. A person should never be appointed to a managerial position if his or her vision focuses on people's weaknesses rather than on their strengths. The aetna's middle layer managers tend to focus on what people cannot do, but never sees anything they can do, that will undermine the spirit of the organization and the senior management's initiatives and objectives that's a huge downside for this organization.

    Advice to Management

    Take a look at the top consumer brands employee development and training and benefits compare and contrast to the Aetna offering since companies goal is to become a consumer preferred it's probably a good idea to look at proctor and gamble see how the employee benefits, training, professional development programs match up to the Aetna offering.
